# Moat Assessment Checklist

A structured evaluation of competitive advantage durability. Work through all five moat types, then score overall. A strong moat requires at least one "Yes" on primary indicators and no fatal vulnerabilities.

---

## The Five Sources of Moat

### 1. Pricing Power (Brand / Intangible Assets)

Primary test: **Can the company raise prices every year without losing meaningful volume?**

- [ ] Has the company raised prices above inflation over the past 10 years?
- [ ] Do customers choose the product partly for what it represents, not just what it does?
- [ ] Would a 10% price increase cause customers to switch, or grumble and pay?
- [ ] Is the brand associated with trust, quality, or identity (not just price competitiveness)?
- [ ] Are there patents, licenses, or regulatory approvals that competitors cannot easily replicate?

**Pricing Power Evidence:**
- Gross margin trend (10Y): ____% → ____%  [Expanding = good signal]
- Last price increase: _____ Amount: ____% Customer reaction: _______
- Competitor pricing vs. this company: _______

**Score (0–3):** ___
*0 = no pricing power, 3 = exceptional (can raise prices freely)*

---

### 2. Switching Costs

Primary test: **Would it be painful, expensive, or risky for a customer to switch to a competitor?**

- [ ] Are customers deeply integrated with the product (data, workflows, training)?
- [ ] Does switching require significant time, money, or retraining?
- [ ] Are there contractual lock-ins or multi-year agreements?
- [ ] Does the product improve the longer a customer uses it (data accumulation, customization)?
- [ ] Do customers voice high satisfaction with service, despite not-best-in-class product?

**Switching Cost Evidence:**
- Net Revenue Retention rate (SaaS): ____%  [>110% = excellent]
- Customer churn rate: ____%/year
- Average customer tenure: _____ years
- Customer concentration (top 10 customers = ___% of revenue)

**Score (0–3):** ___

---

### 3. Network Effects

Primary test: **Does the product become more valuable as more people use it?**

- [ ] Is the product used to interact with other users (marketplace, communication, data)?
- [ ] Would losing 20% of users significantly reduce value for remaining users?
- [ ] Do new users join primarily because existing users are already there?
- [ ] Are there cross-side network effects (e.g., more buyers attract more sellers)?
- [ ] Has the network grown organically without heavy marketing spend?

**Network Effect Evidence:**
- User growth without proportional marketing spend increase: Y/N
- Multi-homing behavior (do users also use competitors?): Y/N
- Geographic or segment "winner-take-most" dynamic: Y/N

**Score (0–3):** ___

---

### 4. Cost Advantage

Primary test: **Can the company deliver the same product/service at materially lower cost than competitors?**

- [ ] Scale advantage: does size reduce unit costs in a way competitors can't match?
- [ ] Unique resource access: proprietary deposits, locations, or supplier relationships?
- [ ] Process advantage: superior operational efficiency that is difficult to replicate?
- [ ] Vertical integration advantage: ownership of key inputs?
- [ ] Is the cost advantage growing or narrowing?

**Cost Advantage Evidence:**
- COGS as % of revenue vs. best competitor: ___% vs. ___%
- Operating margin vs. industry median: ___% vs. ___%
- Has the gap widened or narrowed over 5 years? _______

**Score (0–3):** ___

---

### 5. Efficient Scale

Primary test: **Does the market size support only a few players, limiting rational competitive entry?**

- [ ] Is the market large enough to be attractive but small enough to deter massive entry?
- [ ] Would a new entrant destroy the economics for everyone (natural monopoly logic)?
- [ ] Are there infrastructure or regulatory constraints that limit the number of viable competitors?
- [ ] Does the incumbent enjoy stable, predictable market share?

**Score (0–3):** ___

---

## Moat Durability Assessment

Even a wide moat can be eroded. Check for threats:

### Technological Disruption Risk
- [ ] Is the core product at risk from AI, software, or a different delivery model?
- [ ] Has any incumbent in this industry been disrupted in the last 20 years?
- [ ] Is the company investing in its own disruption (or ignoring it)?

**Disruption Risk: Low / Medium / High**

### Competitive Response Risk
- [ ] Is a well-funded competitor explicitly targeting this company's core customers?
- [ ] Is the moat narrowing (margins compressing, churn rising, pricing power declining)?
- [ ] Has the company lost market share in any key segment in the last 3 years?

**Competitive Response Risk: Low / Medium / High**

### Regulatory / Political Risk
- [ ] Is the business model dependent on a specific regulatory environment?
- [ ] Could antitrust action materially restrict the company?
- [ ] Is there bipartisan political will to constrain this industry?

**Regulatory Risk: Low / Medium / High**
