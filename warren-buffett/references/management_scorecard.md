# Management Quality Scorecard

Buffett: "When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for bad economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact." But great economics with mediocre management also disappoints. This scorecard evaluates management as stewards of shareholder capital.

---

## Part 1: Capital Allocation Track Record

The most important management skill. What have they done with free cash flow over the last 5–10 years?

### Retained Earnings Test
**"The test of whether retained earnings are being wisely deployed is whether each dollar retained over time creates at least one dollar of market value."**

```
Retained Earnings Test:
  Earnings retained over 5 years: $______
  Change in market cap over same 5 years: $______
  Ratio (market cap increase / retained earnings): ______x
  Target: > 1.0x (ideally > 1.5x)
```

**Buyback Quality Check:**
- Did they buy back shares when the stock was cheap (P/E < 15x) or expensive (P/E > 25x)?
- If mostly bought at high prices: subtract points. If bought at low prices: add points.
- Share count change over 5 years: ___% [negative = accretive buybacks]

**Acquisition Quality Check:**
- Last 3 major acquisitions: _______
- Did acquired businesses earn > cost of capital within 3 years? Y/N
- Was goodwill written down afterward? Y/N [write-downs signal overpaid]
- Did management describe synergies that materialized? Y/N

*1 = destroys capital, 5 = exceptional allocator*

## Part 2: Candor and Integrity

Buffett: "Look for three things: intelligence, energy, and integrity. If they don't have the last one, the first two will kill you."

### Annual Report / Letter Test
Read the last 5 years of annual reports and answer:

- [ ] Do they openly discuss mistakes and failures, or only successes?
- [ ] Do they explain the business in plain language, or obscure with jargon?
- [ ] Are forward guidance and actual results consistently aligned?
- [ ] Do they describe the business as owners, or as PR professionals?
- [ ] When something went wrong, did they take responsibility or blame external factors?

### Insider Ownership and Alignment
- CEO ownership of shares (not options): ___% of company / $___M value
- Did CEO buy shares in the open market in the last 3 years? Y/N
- Insider buying vs. selling ratio (last 3 years): ___ buys / ___ sells
- Does compensation align with long-term shareholder returns (TSR) or short-term metrics?

**Red flags:**
- [ ] Frequent restatements of earnings
- [ ] Auditor changes without explanation
- [ ] Related-party transactions benefiting executives
- [ ] Management selling shares while making bullish public statements
- [ ] Guidance that is consistently optimistic vs. actuals

**Candor & Integrity Score (1–5):** ___

## Part 4: Long-Term Orientation

Buffett won't invest with managers who are focused on the next quarter at the expense of the next decade.

- [ ] Does management make explicit long-term commitments (multi-year investment cycles)?
- [ ] Have they maintained R&D/capex investment during downturns, or cut for short-term EPS?
- [ ] Do they provide guidance ranges or refuse to provide quarterly guidance?
- [ ] Have they resisted activist pressure to cut long-term investments for short-term returns?
- [ ] Is there a succession plan? Is key-person risk managed?
