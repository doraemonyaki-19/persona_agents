# 25-Bias Stress Test Worksheet

Apply to any investment thesis, business decision, or analysis. For each bias: (1) is it present? (2) how does it distort the conclusion? (3) what's the corrective?

Source: Munger's "The Psychology of Human Misjudgment" speech, Harvard Law, 1995 (in Poor Charlie's Almanack).

---

## Instructions

Work through all 25. Mark each: **Active** (distorting current analysis), **Potential** (worth watching), or **Not present**.

---

### 1. Reward and Punishment Superresponse Tendency
*Incentives drive behavior more powerfully than rationality, intentions, or training.*

**Check:** Who benefits financially if this thesis is correct? Who loses?
- Is the analyst/manager/banker paid to believe this thesis? ___
- Are management incentives aligned with what they're claiming? ___
- What would this person do if incentives were different? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Distortion:** _______
**Corrective:** Map every incentive structure before trusting any assertion.

---

### 2. Liking/Loving Bias
*We favor people/companies we like and ignore their faults.*

**Check:** Am I attracted to the charismatic CEO? The admirable brand? The clever narrative?
- Would I reach the same conclusion about this business with a mediocre CEO? ___
- Am I dismissing red flags because I "believe in" the company? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Distortion:** _______
**Corrective:** Explicitly list the three best arguments against the thesis before committing.

---

### 3. Disliking/Hating Bias
*We distort facts to harm or dismiss those we dislike.*

**Check:** Am I short this stock partly because I dislike the CEO personally?
- Am I refusing to see genuine strengths because I've already decided this is bad? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Distortion:** _______

---

### 4. Doubt-Avoidance Tendency
*We remove uncertainty by making quick decisions, especially under social pressure.*

**Check:** Am I rushing to a conclusion to end the discomfort of not knowing?
- Has anyone pressured me to have a view before I'm ready? ___
- Is "I don't know" a legitimate answer here, and have I considered it? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Sit with uncertainty. "I don't know" is an honest and valuable position.

---

### 5. Inconsistency-Avoidance Tendency
*We resist changing our minds to avoid admitting prior error.*

**Check:** Am I defending an earlier thesis because changing it would be embarrassing?
- Has new evidence emerged that I've been explaining away? ___
- When did I last change my mind about this company? Why? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Track prior positions. Reward yourself for updating on evidence, not for being consistent.

---

### 6. Envy / Jealousy Tendency
*Envy causes us to take risks we wouldn't otherwise take to avoid being "left behind."*

**Check:** Am I buying this because others are making money on it and I feel left out?
- Is FOMO a component of the thesis? ___
- Would I be interested in this investment if no one I knew owned it? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 7. Reciprocation Tendency
*We feel obligated to return favors — including from sell-side analysts who gave us access.*

**Check:** Am I giving favorable treatment to an idea because the analyst gave me access or did me a favor?

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 8. Influence-from-Mere-Association Tendency
*We associate the qualities of a good past experience with a new, unrelated one.*

**Check:** Am I bullish on Company B because it reminds me of a great experience with Company A?
- Is this a "looks like Amazon in 2003" argument? Is it actually like Amazon, or just superficially similar? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Decompose the analogy. What specifically is similar and what is different?

---

### 9. Simple Pain/Pleasure-Avoiding Psychological Denial
*We distort reality to avoid painful truths.*

**Check:** Is there a fact about this business I've been avoiding looking at directly?
- What is the single most unflattering thing that is true about this investment? ___
- Have I read the bear case in detail, or only glanced at it? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 10. Excessive Self-Regard Tendency
*We systematically overestimate our own abilities and the quality of our analysis.*

**Check:** Am I confident because the analysis is genuinely strong, or because I did the analysis?
- Have I had an independent, skeptical party review this thesis? ___
- What is the base rate for being right on theses like this? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Use base rates. If most stock picks in this category underperform, start from humility.

---

### 11. Overoptimism Tendency
*We systematically forecast better outcomes than base rates support.*

**Check:** Is my projection in the top quartile of outcomes for businesses in this situation?
- What is the median outcome for businesses facing this same set of conditions? ___
- Have I modeled a scenario where nothing goes right? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Anchor forecasts to base rates before adjusting for company-specific factors.

---

### 12. Deprival-Superreaction Tendency (Loss Aversion)
*Losses feel approximately 2x worse than equivalent gains feel good. We hold losers too long.*

**Check:** Am I holding this position because I hate to realize a loss?
- Would I buy this security today at the current price if I didn't own it? ___
- The "Would I buy it fresh?" test: ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Apply the "fresh buy" test to every existing position periodically.

---

### 13. Social-Proof Tendency
*We assume something is correct because many others believe it. Dangerous in markets.*

**Check:** Is the bull case primarily built on the fact that smart people own this?
- What is the consensus view? Is being long/short this position contrarian or consensus? ___
- If the consensus is correct, is there still enough upside to justify the risk? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 14. Contrast-Misreaction Tendency
*We judge things relative to what preceded them, not on absolute terms.*

**Check:** Does this look cheap only because it was recently much more expensive?
- Is "down 50% from the high" a reason to buy, or a red flag? ___
- Compare to absolute value metrics, not to prior price levels. ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 15. Stress-Influence Tendency
*Decision quality degrades significantly under stress or time pressure.*

**Check:** Am I making this decision under artificial urgency?
- Is there a real deadline, or have I created artificial pressure? ___
- Would I make the same decision after a night's sleep? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 16. Availability-Misweighting Tendency
*We overweight vivid, recent, or easily recalled events vs. base rates.*

**Check:** Am I overweighting the last recession, the last great trade, the last earnings call?
- What is the 20-year base rate for this type of business outcome? ___
- Am I being overly cautious because of a vivid recent loss? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 17. Authority-Misinfluence Tendency
*We defer too readily to authority figures, even outside their domain of competence.*

**Check:** Is "Warren Buffett owns it" or "Sequoia backs it" doing real analytical work in my thesis?
- Does the authority figure have genuine relevant expertise here? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 18. Twaddle Tendency
*We fill space with empty talk and accept it from others.*

**Check:** Is the thesis full of vague assertions that can't be tested?
- Can I express the core thesis in one concrete, testable sentence? ___
- Strip out all filler. What is the actual, falsifiable claim? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present
**Corrective:** Require every key assertion to be specific and falsifiable.

---

### 19. Commitment and Consistency Bias
*We feel compelled to act consistently with our prior commitments, even when wrong.*

**Check:** Have I publicly committed to this thesis in a way that makes reversing embarrassing?
- Am I adding more evidence to support a prior conclusion rather than testing it? ___

**Status:** Active / Potential / Not present

---

### 20. Lollapalooza Risk (Multiple Biases Converging)
*When 3+ biases all push in the same direction, outcomes are non-linear.*

**Check:** Which biases from above are ALL pointing toward the same conclusion?
- Active biases favoring the bull case: ___
- Active biases favoring the bear case: ___
- Is there a confluence of biases creating an extreme (overpriced or underpriced) situation? ___
