# Inversion Checklist

Munger: "Invert, always invert. Turn a situation or problem upside down. Look at it backwards."
Source: Attributed to mathematician Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi ("man muss immer umkehren"); applied to business and investing by Munger throughout his career.

The goal is not to find reasons to be bullish. The goal is to find reliable paths to failure — then avoid them. If you can't articulate the failure modes clearly, you don't understand the situation.

---

## Part 1: Invert the Investment Thesis

### The Failure Modes Question
**"What would guarantee that this investment loses money?"**

Work through each failure mode systematically:

#### Business-Level Failures
- [ ] The moat erodes (which competitor, which technology, which regulatory change?)
  - Most likely vector: _______
  - Timeline: _______
  - Probability: ____%

- [ ] Management destroys capital (how? acquisitions? empire-building? fraud?)
  - Most likely form: _______
  - Red flags already visible: _______

- [ ] Revenue declines (why? customer loss? pricing pressure? disruption?)
  - Most likely mechanism: _______
  - Leading indicators to watch: _______

- [ ] The cost structure becomes uncompetitive (why? labor? materials? technology?)
  - _______

- [ ] Leverage becomes fatal during a downturn
  - Current net debt / EBITDA: ___x
  - Debt maturity schedule: _______
  - Covenant triggers: _______

#### Valuation-Level Failures
- [ ] The multiple compresses (from ___x to ___x = ___% price decline even with flat earnings)
- [ ] Earnings normalize down to intrinsic value (what is the normalized earnings power?)
- [ ] The "story" proves to be narrative, not durable economics

#### Macro/Exogenous Failures
- [ ] Interest rate rise destroys the DCF value (if discount rate goes from 8% to 11%, IV = ?)
- [ ] Regulatory intervention (antitrust, price controls, environmental)
- [ ] Black swan (pandemic, war, systemic financial crisis)

## Part 3: Invert the Process

**"What would guarantee that my analysis is wrong?"**

- [ ] I only read bullish sources / analyst reports
- [ ] I have not read the short thesis (if one exists)
- [ ] I have not spoken to a knowledgeable bear
- [ ] My model uses management guidance as inputs without independent verification
- [ ] I have not stress-tested the model with inputs 30% worse than my base case
- [ ] I have a prior commitment to this thesis (published, told others, etc.)

## Part 5: The Avoid List

Munger's most important tool: knowing what to avoid is more reliable than knowing what to pursue.

**For this investment, the things that would make me immediately sell/pass:**

| Trigger | Current Status |
|---------|---------------|
| Val_loss equivalent: key metric falls below ___ | |
| Management does ___ (acquisition > $___, SBC > ___%) | |
| Competitive event: ___ enters the market | |
| Earnings deterioration: gross margin < ___% for 2 consecutive quarters | |
| Balance sheet: net debt > ___x EBITDA | |
| Governance: ___ | |

**Munger's rule**: decide in advance what would make you sell. Don't decide in the moment when emotions are high.
