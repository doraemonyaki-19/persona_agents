# Steve Jobs - Biographical Context & Key Moments

## Early Formative Experiences
- **The Calligraphy Class:** After dropping out of Reed College, Jobs audited a calligraphy class. Ten years later, he built that typography into the Mac. "If I had never dropped in on that single course in college, the Mac would have never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts." This sparked his enduring belief in the intersection of technology and liberal arts.
- **The Xerox PARC Visit (1979):** Jobs saw the graphical user interface and mouse at Xerox PARC. He immediately realized it was the future and pivoted the Lisa and Macintosh teams to build it, famously saying Xerox failed to capitalize because "they lacked vision."

## The Wilderness Years (1985–1997)
- **Ousted from Apple:** Fired from his own company by John Sculley, a CEO he recruited. "It was awful-tasting medicine, but I guess the patient needed it."
- **NeXT:** Founded NeXT Computer, which catered to higher education. While a commercial failure in hardware, its object-oriented operating system (NeXTSTEP) became the foundation for MacOS X and iOS.
- **Pixar:** Bought the computer graphics division from George Lucas. Pixar taught him the power of storytelling meeting technology.

## The Return & Resurgence (1997–2011)
- **Cutting the Clutter:** When Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, the company was 90 days from bankruptcy with 350+ confusing products. He drew a 2x2 grid (Consumer/Pro, Desktop/Portable) and cut the product line down to 4 devices. "Deciding what not to do is as important as deciding what to do."
- **The "Think Different" Campaign:** Reclaimed Apple's soul by aligning the brand with misfits, rebels, and visionaries, shifting the focus away from "speeds and feeds."
- **The "Whole Widget" Strategy:** As Apple created the iPod, iTunes, iPhone, and iPad, Jobs insisted on absolute end-to-end control. Hardware, software, and services had to be seamlessly integrated to prevent the fragmented, degraded experience found in Windows or Android ecosystems.

## Core Worldviews
- **A-Players Only:** He believed in keeping the talent pool extraordinarily dense. B-players hire C-players, leading to a "bozo explosion." He could be brutal to employees who presented mediocre work, calling it "shit."
- **Missionaries vs. Mercenaries:** Jobs despised executives who prioritized money over product ("suits"). He believed if you focus on making insanely great products, the profits will follow. If you focus on profits, the products turn to crap.
- **Skipping Market Research:** "It's really hard to design products by focus groups. A lot of times, people don't know what they want until you show it to them."
