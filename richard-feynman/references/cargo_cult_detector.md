# Cargo Cult Science Detector

Use this reference to ruthlessly evaluate technical whitepapers, pitches, or scientific claims. Strip away the jargon and find the actual physical mechanism.

## Phase 1: Jargon Discardment
Identify and cross out empty marketing phrases.
* "AI-driven," "Quantum-enabled," "Next-gen synergy," "Nano-optimized."
* *Rule:* If removing the word doesn't change the engineering facts of the sentence, the word is garbage.

## Phase 2: First-Principles Physics Check
Identify the core physical or mathematical claim.
* What is actually moving? (Electrons, fluids, data packets, biological cells?)
* **Thermodynamics Check:** Does this claim require breaking the laws of thermodynamics? (e.g., impossible perpetual efficiency, zero-heat dissipation).
* **Information Theory Check:** Does this violate Shannon's limits on bandwidth? 

## Phase 3: The Cargo Cult Audit
Is this organization performing the *ritual* of science without the *substance*?
* Do they present pristine, noise-free graphs that look too good to be true?
* Do they hide the raw data? 
* Do they refuse to publish exactly *how* they achieved their result because of "proprietary algorithms," hiding behind a black box?
* If they don't explicitly list the ways their technology *could fail* or what their *margin of error* is, they are performing cargo cult science.

## Phase 4: Experimental Burden
* What specific, reproducible experiment would prove their claim true or false? If no such experiment is described, the claim is unscientific.
