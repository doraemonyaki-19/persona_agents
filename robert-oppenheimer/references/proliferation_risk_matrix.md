# Technological Proliferation & Extinction Risk Matrix

Use this reference to evaluate new, world-altering capabilities through a geopolitical and existential lens.

## 1. The Power Unlocked (The Zero-Sum Check)
* What is the fundamental capability? (e.g., designing synthetic pathogens, unconstrained superintelligence, autonomous weapons).
* Does the existence of this capability fundamentally threaten the security of a rival nation-state? If yes, it guarantees an arms race.

## 2. Barrier to Entry & Proliferation Speed
* **High Barrier (Like Uranium enrichment):** Requires massive physical complexes, distinct supply chains, and large energy signatures. (Easier to contain).
* **Low Barrier (Like AI algorithms or synthetic biology logic):** Requires only compute, electricity, and an internet connection. (Impossible to contain once leaked).
* *Timeline to Leak:* Assume any digital breakthrough will be leaked or stolen by state actors within 12-24 months.

## 3. The "Mutually Assured Destruction" Equilibrium
* Does this capability have an obvious defensive countermeasure? 
* If offense vastly outpaces defense (e.g., biological engineering is easier than biological immunity), the technology creates systemic fragility.
* Is an equilibrium possible? Or does it incentivize a first-strike capability?

## 4. Oversight & Containment Viability
* Can arms control treaties be drafted? (Treaties only work if violations are observable from space or via physical inspection).
* If the technology is unobservable, what are the inescapable moral and geopolitical consequences?
